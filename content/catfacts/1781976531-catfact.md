# 5 Fascinating Feline Facts

Welcome to the first automated installment of feline knowledge! Here are five purr-fectly interesting facts about cats:

1. **A unique purr:** A cat's purr doesn't always mean they are happy. Cats also purr when they are stressed, in pain, or giving birth. The frequency of a cat's purr (between 25 and 150 Hertz) has been shown to improve bone density and promote tissue healing.
2. **Speedy sprinters:** Despite their reputation for lounging around all day, cats are incredibly fast runners. They can reach speeds of up to 30 mph over short distances, making them formidable hunters in short bursts.
3. **No collarbones:** Cats do not have a rigid collarbone (clavicle) like humans do. Instead, it is attached by muscle, which allows them to squeeze through remarkably tight spaces. A good rule of thumb is that if a cat's head can fit through an opening, their body likely can too.
4. **Tastebud trivia:** Unlike most mammals, cats are completely lacking the ability to taste sweetness. A mutation in a key taste receptor gene means they have no interest in sugary treats (though they might still be interested in the fat or protein content of a sweet dessert).
5. **A tower of whiskers:** Whiskers aren't just on a cat's face! They also have a specialized set of whiskers on the back of their front legs, called carpal whiskers. These are highly sensitive and help them detect the movement of prey that they have caught in their paws.
