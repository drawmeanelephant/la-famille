# 5 Fascinating Facts About Historical Cats

1. **The Mayor of Talkeetna:** A ginger tabby cat named Stubbs served as the honorary mayor of Talkeetna, Alaska, for 20 years (from 1997 to 2017). He was elected in a write-in campaign by voters who were unhappy with the human candidates.
2. **The Only Cat to go to Space:** On October 18, 1963, a French stray cat named Félicette became the first and only cat to survive a trip to space. She launched aboard a Véronique AG1 rocket and experienced about five minutes of weightlessness.
3. **The Unsinkable Sam:** A cat known as "Unsinkable Sam" (originally named Oscar) reportedly survived the sinking of three different ships during World War II: the German battleship Bismarck, the British destroyer HMS Cossack, and the aircraft carrier HMS Ark Royal.
4. **Isaac Newton's Cat Flap:** The famous physicist Sir Isaac Newton is widely credited with inventing the cat flap. Legend has it he cut two holes in his door—a large one for his adult cat and a smaller one for her kittens—so they wouldn't interrupt his optical experiments by scratching at the door.
5. **The Stationmaster of Kishi:** A calico cat named Tama became the stationmaster and operating officer at Kishi Station on the Kishigawa Line in Kinokawa, Wakayama, Japan. Her tenure popularized the station and brought an estimated 1.1 billion yen to the local economy.
