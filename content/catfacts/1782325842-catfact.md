Here are 5 fascinating facts about historical cats:

1. **Astrocat:** Felicette, a Parisian street cat, was the first and only feline to journey to space. In 1963, she traveled 100 miles above Earth, experiencing five minutes of weightlessness before safely returning via parachute.
2. **The Mayor:** Stubbs, an orange tabby, served as the honorary mayor of Talkeetna, Alaska, for 20 years. He "held office" from 1997 until his passing in 2017, conducting his mayoral duties primarily from a local general store.
3. **Stationmaster:** Tama, a calico cat, saved the struggling Kishi train station in Wakayama, Japan. Appointed as the official stationmaster in 2007, she wore a custom hat and significantly boosted the local economy by attracting tourists.
4. **Unsinkable Sam:** Also known as Oskar, this incredibly lucky cat survived the sinking of three different ships during World War II: the German battleship Bismarck, the HMS Cossack, and the HMS Ark Royal.
5. **The Muse:** The legendary physicist Sir Isaac Newton is widely credited with inventing the cat flap. Frustrated by his cats continually pushing open his door while he was experimenting with light in a darkened room, he had a carpenter cut two holes in the door—a large one for the mother cat and a smaller one for her kittens.
